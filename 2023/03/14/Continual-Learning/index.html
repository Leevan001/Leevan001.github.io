Memory stability

Memory stability can be evaluated by forgetting measure (FM) and backward transfer (BWT)
$$
f_{j, k}=\max {i \in{1, \ldots, k-1}}\left(a{i, j}-a_{k, j}\right), \forall j<k .
$$
FM at the k-th task is the average forgetting of all old tasks:
$$
\mathrm{FM}{k}=\frac{1}{k-1} \sum{j=1}^{k-1} f_{j, k}
$$

$$
\mathrm{BWT}{k}=\frac{1}{k-1} \sum{j=1}^{k-1}\left(a_{k, j}-a_{j, j}\right)
$$

Learning plasticity

Learning plasticity can be evaluated by intransience measure (IM) and forward transfer (FWT)

IM is defined as the inability of a model to learn new tasks, which is calculated by the difference of a task between its joint training performance and continual learning performance:
$$
\mathrm{IM}{k}=a{k}^{}-a_{k, k}
$$
where $a_{k}^{}$is the classification accuracy of a randomly initialized reference model jointly trained with $\cup_{j=1}^{k} \mathcal{D}{j}$ for the k-th task. In comparison, FWT evaluates the average influence of all old tasks on the current k-th task:
$$
\operatorname{FWT}k=\dfrac{1}{k-1}\sum\limits{j=2}^k(a{j,j}-\tilde{a}_j)
$$
